# Minecraft-Texture-Rename🛠️ Minecraft Texture Renamer (The "Cupscale Fixer")
💀 The Problem (Why does this exist?)
You decided to upscale your favorite Minecraft resource pack using Cupscale and an awesome AI model. You waited for hours, your room smelled like burning graphics cards, and your 4GB RAM was praying for mercy.
Finally, it's done! You launch Minecraft, ready to see glorious ultra-HD pixels... and BAM! Everything is a terrifying grid of PURPLE AND BLACK BLOCKS. 😭💀
Why? Because Cupscale decided to be "helpful" and renamed your beloved dirt.png to something like dirt_4x_UltraSharp_Anime_Hyper_Pro_Max_V2.png. Minecraft looks at this name and says: "I don't know her."
✨ The Solution
Meet this ultra-lightweight C++ tool. No bloat, no heavy electron frameworks, just pure speed.
What it does: It takes the original folder with the correct names, takes your messy AI-upscaled folder, and snaps its fingers. Boom. All files renamed perfectly back to what Minecraft expects.
Why C++? Because your RAM deserves a break after Cupscale almost melted your PC. ⚡
🚀 Features
Drag & Drop: No more clicking through folders like it's 1999. Just drag, drop, and conquer.
Advanced Swap Logic: Easily switch between original and AI paths with a single button.
Optimized Workflow: Built for those who have actual work to do, not just waiting for UI animations to load.
⚠️ Very Legal & Binding Condition (Don't break the magic!)
🚨 Crucial Rule: The number of files in the original folder MUST EXACTLY EQUAL the number of files in your AI-upscaled folder.
Why? Because math. If you give the tool 50 original names but your AI folder has 51 files, the tool will look at you with deep confusion.
What happens if they don't match? The tool won't work, and you will be forced to manually count pixels as a punishment. Ensure your AI output didn't drop or add any random files before clicking that big glorious button!
